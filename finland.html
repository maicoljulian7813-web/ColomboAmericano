Introduction

Welcome to Finland

Finland is a country in Northern Europe. It has about 5.6 million people. One famous place is Rovaniemi, with about 65,000 residents. Finland is a clean, modern, and safe country. Finnish people love nature, and they respect silence and order.

Finland is famous for winter sports. People play ice hockey, and many people go skiing and go ice skating in winter.

Sports & Activities

What Can You Do?

⛷

Visit Santa Claus Village & see the Aurora

Tourists can visit Santa Claus Village and see the Aurora Borealis at night. They should sit quietly to enjoy the Northern Lights — and use a smartwatch to track their GPS route.

🏂

Snowboarding, skiing & ice skating

Tourists can go snowboarding, can go skiing, and can go ice skating. They can also ride sleighs and go snowmobiling across the frozen landscape.

📸

Photography & nature walks

Use their eyes to watch the sky. A smartwatch helps check activity and a sports camera captures the moment. Silent snow forests are perfect for photography.

⚠ Rules to Remember

  • Tourists should move carefully and act safely on ice and snow.
  • They can't throw garbage in nature or natural areas.
  • They can't disturb wild animals — including reindeer.

Eco-Friendly Tips

Be Kind to Nature 🌿

💧

Tourists should carry a reusable bottle — the tap water in Finland is very clean and drinkable.

🚌

They could use the local bus or electric transport to reduce pollution in the city.

🌲

They should respect wild animals and forests. Stay on marked paths and avoid disturbing nature.

🚶

They could walk in the city or use electric transport — Rovaniemi is compact and walkable.

🚫

They can't throw garbage in natural places — Finnish culture takes environmental respect very seriously.

👗 What to Wear

Tourists should wear warm clothes and waterproof clothes because the weather is very cold in winter. Temperatures can drop to −30°C in Lapland. Thermal layers, gloves, and snow boots are essential.

Frequently Asked Questions

Finland FAQ ❓

When do the Northern Lights appear?

Usually between 9:00 p.m. and 2:00 a.m. in winter months. September through March is the best window.

Can I walk safely at night?

Yes! Tourists can walk safely at night in Rovaniemi. The city is safe and well-lit even in winter darkness.

Should I bring warm and waterproof clothes?

Yes, they should, because the temperature is very low — sometimes below −20°C in Lapland.

Can I pay with a contactless card?

Yes, tourists can pay for public transport and most services with a contactless card. Finland is very digital.

Is the tap water drinkable?

Yes! Finnish tap water is one of the cleanest in the world. A reusable bottle is enough.

How far is Santa Claus Village?

About 8 kilometers from central Rovaniemi, or 15 minutes by bus from the city center.

Where do I buy tickets for activities?

Tourists can buy tickets online, at tourist information points, or at the activity center in Rovaniemi.

How much money for activities?

Tourists usually spend about 50 to 150 euros per activity, depending on the experience.
