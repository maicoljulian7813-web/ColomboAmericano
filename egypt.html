Introduction

Cairo is the biggest Arab city; 22 million residents are living here in the urban zone. The residents are young and dynamic. Cairo is the artistic and intellectual center of the region. The people are very friendly, and the Nile River is the heart of the city.

Soccer Culture

02

Eco-Friendly Tips

What kind of places would I visit?

01

Pyramids & Desert

In Cairo you can see the pyramids and the desert. You need to wear light clothes because it is very warm, and you need to stay fresh for different activities.

02

Camel Rides

In this city you can ride a camel, but carefully because this animal is tall and fast. You ride with a guide safely.

Section 4 of 5

Gastronomy & Food

What kind of food should I try?

Main Dish

Koshary

Mixed with rice, pasta, lentils, and spiced tomato sauce with crispy onions.

Street Food

Ta'ameya

Egyptian falafel, made from fava beans and fresh herbs, giving it a vibrant green color inside.

Dessert

Om Ali

A star dessert — pudding with puff pastry, milk, and nuts. This dish is eaten hot.

Section 5 of 5

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take a taxi to the Pyramids?

Taxis are more expensive. You can take a bus — it is easy and cheap.

Should women cover their shoulders and heads?

Yes, it is mandatory in mosques and respectful in the city.

Should I pay in local currency?

Yes, most monuments only accept cards or EGP (Egyptian Pounds).

Where can I buy a SIM card?

At the airport or at Vodafone/Orange stores in the city.

Are photos allowed in the tombs?

Sometimes, but it usually requires an extra ticket.
