Introduction

Welcome to Egypt

Egypt is a country in North Africa. Its capital city is Cairo, with about 22 million residents. Egypt is famous for history, culture, and art. The people are friendly, and the Nile River is very important in the city.

Egypt is also famous for soccer. Many people play soccer, and it is a very popular sport in the country. Mohamed Salah is one of the most famous Egyptian football players in the world.

Sports & Activities

What Can You Do?

🏛

Visit the Pyramids & explore the desert

Tourists can visit the pyramids, explore the desert, ride a camel, and can also go sandboarding. They use their legs and feet to keep balance on sand dunes.

🐫

Camel rides at sunset

One of the most memorable experiences in Cairo. Tourists should be careful — the animal is tall, strong, and fast. Always ride with a guide.

📱

Track your adventure

Tourists could use a fitness tracker to check their physical effort. A sports camera is great for capturing pyramid views. They use their arms to control movement on camels.

⚠ Rules to Remember

  • Tourists should be careful — the weather is very hot.
  • They can't take pictures without permission inside monuments.
  • They can't mistreat animals — camels and horses must be respected.
  • They should act respectfully in mosques and cultural places.

Eco-Friendly Tips

Respect & Protect 🌍

🏛

Tourists should respect monuments, animals, and local culture — these places are thousands of years old.

🚫

They should not throw garbage in the desert or near historical places — littering is disrespectful and harmful.

🚌

They could use organized transport and travel with a local guide to reduce environmental impact.

💧

They should drink water frequently to stay healthy in the heat. Dehydration is a real risk in Cairo.

👣

They should walk carefully in the desert — the sand is unstable and the terrain can be dangerous.

👗 What to Wear

Tourists should wear light clothes because the weather is warm — sometimes above 38°C. Breathable fabrics, sunglasses, a hat, and sun protection are essential. They should cover shoulders in mosques as a sign of respect.

Frequently Asked Questions

Egypt FAQ ❓

Can I take a taxi to the pyramids?

Yes, tourists can, but the bus is cheaper and easier. Ride-share apps like Uber also work well in Cairo.

Should women cover their shoulders and head?

Yes, they should in mosques. Covering shoulders is respectful in many cultural and historical places throughout Cairo.

Should I pay in local currency?

Yes, tourists should use Egyptian pounds for most local transactions. ATMs are widely available.

Where can I buy a SIM card?

Tourists can buy a SIM card at the airport or in Vodafone and Orange stores throughout Cairo.

How long does it take to get to Cairo?

The trip can take about 16 to 22 hours from South America, depending on the route and connections.

Are photos allowed inside the tombs?

Sometimes yes, but often an extra ticket is necessary. Always ask before photographing in restricted areas.

How many pyramids are in Giza?

There are three main pyramids in Giza: Khufu (the Great Pyramid), Khafre, and Menkaure.

How much water should I carry in the desert?

Tourists need a lot of water — at least 2–3 liters per day. The weather is very hot and dehydration is a serious risk.
